Health Education Exercise Answers

A. Very Short Answers

a. What do you mean by sex?
Sex is defined as the biological differences between males and females based on their body parts and chromosomes.
b. How is your sexual identity determined?
Sexual identity is determined by our feelings, attractions, and how we understand ourselves as individuals.
c. List out the different types of sexual identity.
The different types include heterosexual, homosexual, bisexual, asexual, and pansexual identities.
d. Which is the most common sexual identity?
Heterosexuality is considered the most common sexual identity.
e. What is homosexuality?
Homosexuality is defined as the romantic or sexual attraction between people of the same gender.
f. Why is it necessary to teach young people about sexual education? Give two reasons
First reason is to help them understand their changing bodies. Second reason is to protect them from abuse and inappropriate behavior.
g. What is sexual abuse?
Sexual abuse is defined as any unwanted or inappropriate sexual behavior done to someone without their permission.
h. How does involving in interactive activities help with safe sexual behaviors?
Interactive activities help by making learning more interesting and helping students remember important safety information better.
i. Sexuality education should be given to adolescents. Why?
Sexuality education should be given because adolescents are going through physical and emotional changes and need proper guidance.

B. Short Answers

a. Name the things that fall under sexuality.
The things that fall under sexuality include physical attraction, emotional feelings, relationships, gender identity, and reproductive health.
b. What is the main difference between Asexual and Bisexual people?
Asexual people are defined as those who do not feel sexual attraction to others. Bisexual people are defined as those who feel attraction to both males and females.
c. "Sexual education should be taught during adolescence". Why do you think that is?
Sexual education should be taught during adolescence because this is the time when young people experience physical and emotional changes. They need proper knowledge to understand these changes and make healthy decisions.
d. Why do women have periods? Explain in a couple of sentences.
Women have periods because their body prepares for pregnancy every month. When pregnancy doesn't happen, the lining of the uterus sheds, which is called menstruation.
e. How should women maintain personal hygiene during their periods? Mention any two ways.
First way is to change sanitary pads regularly every 4-6 hours. Second way is to wash hands properly before and after changing pads and keep the genital area clean.
f. List down one change each that girls and boys undergo during puberty.
Girls undergo the change of starting menstruation. Boys undergo the change of voice becoming deeper.
g. "Young adolescents need proper counselling regarding sexual behaviors" Can you give any two reasons why?
First reason is to help them understand what behaviors are appropriate and safe. Second reason is to protect them from making wrong decisions that could harm them.
h. In a couple of points explain why adolescent friendly sexual and reproductive health services are necessary.
These services are necessary because young people need safe spaces to ask questions without fear. They also need accurate information from trained professionals who understand their specific needs.

C. Long Answers

a. There is a big debate concerning gender identity. In your opinion what is gender? How many genders are there? Give a couple of reasons to back your claims.
Gender is defined as how a person identifies themselves mentally and socially. There are mainly two genders - male and female. First reason is that biology shows two main types based on body structure. Second reason is that most societies recognize these two primary gender roles.
b. Why do people of different sexual orientation feel difficult to come out? Give any two reasons. Mention any two things that you can do to make it easier for them to live comfortably.
First reason is fear of family rejection. Second reason is worry about society's negative treatment. To help them: we can treat everyone with equal respect, and we can avoid making jokes or comments about different orientations.
c. What are menstruations/Periods? Women feel a lot of discomfort during their periods and yet there are restrictions placed by our society on women on their periods. Give any three restrictions that women face during their periods.
Menstruation is defined as the monthly bleeding from uterus when pregnancy doesn't happen. Three restrictions are: not allowed in temples or religious places, cannot touch food items or cook, and must stay separated from normal family activities.
d. Comprehensive sex education services are essential for getting reliable information on sex related matter. In what ways do they benefit teenagers? Explain.
Sex education benefits teenagers by providing correct scientific information instead of myths. It helps them understand body changes during puberty. It teaches them about safety and protection from harmful situations.
e. How do you know that someone is harassing you sexually? How can you prevent yourself from such harassment?
Sexual harassment is identified when someone touches you inappropriately or makes uncomfortable comments about your body. You feel scared or uncomfortable. To prevent it: immediately tell trusted adults, say "NO" loudly and clearly, and avoid unsafe situations or people.
