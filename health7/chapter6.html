Complete Reference for Physical Training & Drills

üîç Give Very Short Answers to These Questions

a. What are physical exercises?
Physical exercises are defined as activities that are planned and performed regularly to improve or maintain physical fitness and overall health.
b. What do you do before doing any sort of exercises?
Before doing any sort of exercises, you must perform warming-up and stretching to prepare the muscles and prevent injury.
c. What is the exercise you do for your chest?
An exercise commonly done for the chest is the Push-up.
d. Name the exercises you do for calves?
The exercises done for the calves are generally called Calf Raises.
e. Who gives the commands during drills?
The commands during drills are typically given by the Physical Training (PT) teacher or an instructor.
f. Why is the "About turn" command given?
The "About turn" command is given to instruct the drill participant to turn 180 degrees to face the opposite direction.
g. At which command do you move forward during a drill?
You move forward during a drill at the command "March" or "Quick March".
h. When is the drill completed?
The drill is completed when the command "Dismiss" or "Shabd" (a local term used for dismissal/end) is given by the instructor.

üìù Give Short Answers to These Questions

a. Give any two ways to stretch your legs before exercise.
Two ways to stretch your legs before exercise are:
1. Quadriceps Stretch:
This is done by holding the foot and pulling it towards the back/buttocks.
2. Hamstring Stretch:
This is done by sitting or standing and reaching towards the toes to feel a stretch along the back of the thigh.
b. In a sentence each, explain the two exercises you do for your legs.
Two exercises done for the legs are:
1. Squats:
This exercise is done by bending the knees and lowering the hips as if sitting in a chair, which strengthens the thighs and glutes.
2. Lunges:
This exercise is done by taking a large step forward and lowering the body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle, which targets each leg individually.
c. Write down any two benefits of drills.
Two benefits of drills are:
  • It builds discipline: Drills teach participants to follow instructions precisely and immediately, which helps build discipline.
  • It promotes teamwork: Performing movements in synchronization requires coordination and attention to others, thereby promoting teamwork and unity.
d. What are students expected to do when "ATTENTION" and "STAND AT EASE" commands are given?
When "ATTENTION" is commanded:
Students are expected to stand firmly with their heels together, body straight, and hands tightly by their sides.
When "STAND AT EASE" is commanded:
Students are expected to step one foot out, keep their hands joined behind their back, and relax slightly while remaining silent.
e. How would you do a "right turn"? Write down a couple of steps.
To perform a "right turn":
  • Step 1 - Pivot: Pivot on the heel of the right foot and the ball of the left foot.
  • Step 2 - Bring feet together: Move the left foot smartly to meet the right foot, returning to the position of attention while facing the new direction.
f. What do you do when the "Eyes right" command is given?
When the "Eyes right" command is given, you turn your head sharply to the right to look towards the reviewing officer or flag, while keeping your body and shoulders straight, and then turn your head back to the front upon receiving the next command (like "Eyes front").

üí° Write Long Answers to These Questions

a. You must have heard a common phrase "Your health is your wealth". Can you give any four reason to back this saying?
The phrase "Your health is your wealth" means that your well-being is the most valuable thing you possess.

Four reasons to back this saying are:
  • Saves Money: Being healthy means you spend less money on doctors, medicines, and hospital visits.
  • Increases Energy: Good health provides the energy and stamina needed to work hard, study effectively, and participate in all daily activities successfully.
  • Ensures Happiness: A healthy body and mind lead to a positive outlook, reducing stress and anxiety, which contributes to overall happiness and a better quality of life.
  • Allows for Work/Study: Only a healthy person can fully utilize their time and talents, allowing them to earn a living or study well to secure their future, which are the real forms of wealth.
b. What is the exercise you do for your chest? How do you do this exercise? Explain in three steps.
The primary exercise done for the chest is the Push-up. This exercise strengthens the chest (pectorals), shoulders, and triceps.

Steps to perform a Push-up:
  • Step 1 - Starting Position: Lie face down on the ground, placing your hands shoulder-width apart and slightly ahead of your shoulders, with your body straight (forming a plank from head to heel).
  • Step 2 - Lowering Phase: Slowly bend your elbows outwards to lower your chest towards the ground until your elbows are at a 90-degree angle. Keep your body in a straight line without letting your hips sag or rise.
  • Step 3 - Pushing Phase: Push the ground away from you by straightening your elbows, returning your body back to the straight starting position. This completes one repetition.
