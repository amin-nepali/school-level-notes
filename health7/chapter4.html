Class 7 chapter 4 Answers — Diseases, safety and First Aid
A. Give very short answers to these questions
a. How do we get diseases?
We get diseases when germs enter our body through dirty food, dirty water, polluted air, or by contact with sick people.
b. What is the main cause of respiratory diseases among people these days?
The main cause of respiratory diseases among people these days is breathing polluted air with smoke and dust.
c. Name any two air borne diseases.
The two air borne diseases are Tuberculosis and Influenza.
d. What causes Whooping cough?
Whooping cough is caused by a bacteria called Bordetella pertussis.
e. Mention the easiest way to prevent sexually transmitted diseases.
The easiest way to prevent sexually transmitted diseases is to avoid unsafe sexual contact and to use clean needles when needed.
f. What is the first symptom shown by a person suffering from Tonsillitis?
The first symptom shown by a person suffering from Tonsillitis is a sore throat.
g. Name a disease similar to Tonsillitis.
A disease similar to Tonsillitis is Pharyngitis.
h. What do you mean by First Aid?
First Aid means the quick help given to an injured or sick person before a doctor arrives.
i. What is a first aid box?
A first aid box is a box that contains medicines and tools for giving first aid.
j. If a person has hit his leg hard on something and can’t move his toes, what could have happened to that person?
He may have fractured his leg or toes.
k. What is the first thing that you should do if you see someone getting an electric shock from a wire?
The first thing to do is to switch off the electricity.
l. How would you know if a person is suffering a shock?
A person suffering a shock may be pale, weak, shivering, or unconscious.
m. In your opinion, who are the people that make roads a dangerous place to ride?
Careless and rash drivers make roads a dangerous place to ride.
B. Give short answers to these questions
a. What is safety and safety education?
Safety means staying away from danger, and safety education means learning how to avoid accidents and keep ourselves safe.
b. What factors cause diseases? List any four.
The factors that cause diseases are dirty food, dirty water, polluted air, and lack of cleanliness.
c. Write any two preventive measures from air borne diseases.
The two preventive measures from air borne diseases are wearing a mask and keeping away from sick people.
d. Mention any two common causes of diseases we get from syringes and blood.
The two common causes are using dirty syringes and unsafe blood transfusion.
e. How can the diseases that get transmitted from infected syringes be prevented?
Diseases from infected syringes can be prevented by always using new, sealed syringes.
f. List any two symptoms shown by a person suffering from STDs.
The two symptoms shown by a person suffering from STDs are sores and itching in the private parts.
g. Name any 4 items that you would put in a first aid box.
The four items we would put in a first aid box are bandages, antiseptic, scissors, and cotton.
h. Mention any two ways a person could get a shock.
A person could get a shock by touching electric wires or wet switches.
i. How do road accidents occur? Give any two reasons.
Road accidents occur due to speeding and careless driving.
j. Why is giving first aid important? Give any two reasons.
Giving first aid is important because it saves lives and prevents injuries from becoming worse.
k. Write any four ways of avoiding snake bites.
The four ways of avoiding snake bites are wearing shoes, avoiding tall grass, using a stick while walking, and keeping the surroundings clean.
Long answer questions
a. "Diseases that transmit from polluted air are very common nowadays." List some diseases that transmit from air along with their causes and preventive measures.
The diseases that transmit from polluted air are Tuberculosis, Influenza, and Pneumonia. The causes are polluted air, dust, and close contact with sick people. The preventive measures are wearing masks, keeping rooms clean, and avoiding crowds.
b. Syringes must be handled with extra care. Do you agree? Also write down the steps of first aid to ensure the syringes are safe.
I agree because dirty syringes can spread diseases like HIV and Hepatitis. The steps to ensure syringes are safe are to always use a new syringe, check the seal, and never share it.
c. Give a step by step instruction in case of electric shock and for a person who has a sprain.
The steps to give first aid in case of electric shock are: turn off the power, use a dry stick to move the person away from the wire, check if they are breathing, and take them to the hospital. The steps to give first aid for a sprain are: rest the injured part, apply ice, wrap with a bandage, and keep it raised.
d. What should you not do to a person who is bitten by a snake? List any two things. What is the correct way of giving it? Give any two methods.
You should not give food or water and you should not cut the wound. The correct ways of giving first aid are to keep the person still, tie above the bite, and take them to the hospital quickly.
