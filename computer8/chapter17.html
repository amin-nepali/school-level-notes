4. Give Reasons (Concise)

  1. Ionic compounds have high melting points.
    Strong **electrostatic forces** hold positive and negative ions together. A large amount of energy is needed to break this crystal lattice structure, resulting in high melting points. (28 words)
  2. Metals are lustrous.
    Free electrons on the surface absorb light and re-emit it, giving metals a characteristic **shining or reflective surface** (metallic lustre). (24 words)
  3. Metals are ductile.
    Layers of atoms can slide over one another when stress is applied, allowing them to be stretched or **drawn into thin wires**. (23 words)
  4. Lead is harmful to humans.
    Lead is a toxic heavy metal that accumulates in the body, causing severe damage to the **nervous system**, kidneys, and blood formation. (25 words)
  5. Metals are good conductors of electricity.
    They possess **mobile or free electrons** that can easily move and carry the electric current throughout the metal structure. (18 words)
  6. Metals are used to make cooking utensils.
    Metals are **good conductors of heat** and have **high melting points**, allowing efficient heat transfer and durability. (21 words)
  7. Electroplating of copper can be made on iron but not on silver.
    Copper is **less reactive than iron**, ensuring a stable coat. It is **more reactive than silver**, making stable plating difficult on silver. (28 words)
  8. Sodium is kept immersed in kerosene oil.
    Sodium is highly reactive. It reacts vigorously with air (oxygen) and moisture, so kerosene oil prevents its contact and stops dangerous reactions. (26 words)

5. & 3(p). Differentiation (Physical Properties)

Property Metals Non-metals
1. Physical State Solids at room temperature (except Mercury). Solids, liquids, or gases exist at room temperature.
2. Lustre Have a **shiny** surface (metallic lustre). Generally **dull** (except Iodine, Graphite).
3. Malleability/Ductility Are **malleable** (sheets) and **ductile** (wires). Are **brittle**; neither malleable nor ductile.
4. Conductivity **Good conductors** of heat and electricity. **Poor conductors** (except Graphite).
5. Density & Hardness Generally have **high density** and are hard. Generally have **low density** and are soft (except Diamond).

2. Short Answer Type Questions

  1. What is metal? Give an example of a shining non-metal and an example of the best conductor of heat.
    Metals are lustrous, malleable, ductile elements; they form cations. Shining non-metal: **Iodine**. Best heat conductor: **Silver**. (25 words)
  2. What is the difference between conductor and insulator?
    **Conductors** allow current/heat flow easily; **insulators** restrict it (e.g., Copper vs. Wood). (18 words)
  3. What is a malleable metal?
    A metal that can be hammered into **thin sheets** without cracking or breaking. (15 words)
  4. What is metallic bond?
    Electrostatic attraction between positive metal ions and the surrounding **sea of mobile electrons**. (15 words)
  5. Name any two soft metals.
    **Sodium** ($\text{Na}$) and **Potassium** ($\text{K}$) are soft. (8 words)
  6. Which non-metal can conduct electricity?
    **Graphite** (an allotrope of Carbon). (6 words)
  7. What are the metals found as minerals in human body?
    **Iron**, **Calcium**, **Sodium**, **Potassium**, **Magnesium**, and **Zinc**. (9 words)
  8. What is metallic lustre?
    The characteristic **shininess** of metals due to the reflection of light by free electrons. (14 words)
  9. Write any four plant sources of iron in human body.
    Spinach, Lentils, Beans, and Tofu (or Dried Apricots). (7 words)
  10. Name two metals which are harmful to human beings.
    **Lead** ($\text{Pb}$) and **Mercury** ($\text{Hg}$) are highly toxic. (8 words)

3. Long Answer Type Questions

  1. Enlist any five properties of metals and non-metals each.
    **Metals:** Lustrous, Malleable, Ductile, Good Conductors, Form Basic Oxides. **Non-metals:** Dull, Brittle, Non-ductile, Poor Conductors, Form Acidic Oxides. (24 words)
  2. Give an example of metal which: i) is a liquid at room temperature. ii) is a poor conductor of electricity. iii) can be easily cut with a knife.
    i) **Mercury** ($\text{Hg}$); ii) **Lead** ($\text{Pb}$); iii) **Sodium** ($\text{Na}$) or **Potassium** ($\text{K}$). (10 words)
  3. What would you observe when zinc is added to a dilute hydrochloric acid? Write the chemical reaction that takes place.
    **Observation:** Rapid bubbling (effervescence) of **Hydrogen gas** occurs. **Reaction:** $\text{Zn}(s) + 2\text{HCl}(aq) \longrightarrow \text{ZnCl}_2(aq) + \text{H}_2(g)$. (24 words)
  4. Write the equation for the reaction of: i) calcium and water ii) sodium and oxygen iii) magnesium and hydrochloric acid
    i) $\text{Ca} + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} \longrightarrow \text{Ca}(\text{OH})_2 + \text{H}_2$. ii) $4\text{Na} + \text{O}_2 \longrightarrow 2\text{Na}_2\text{O}$. iii) $\text{Mg} + 2\text{HCl} \longrightarrow \text{MgCl}_2 + \text{H}_2$. (19 words)
  5. An element reacts with oxygen to give a compound with a high melting point. This compound is also soluble in water. Which element - calcium or carbon is it likely to be?
    It is likely **Calcium** ($\text{Ca}$). It forms **Calcium Oxide** ($\text{CaO}$), an ionic compound with a high melting point and good water solubility ($\text{Ca}(\text{OH})_2$). (27 words)
  6. There are three materials $\text{A}$, $\text{B}$ and $\text{C}$. Materials $\text{A}$ and $\text{C}$ are soft and $\text{B}$ is lustrous and hard; but material $\text{B}$ is dull and not very hard. Identify the metals and non-metals from $\text{A}$, $\text{B}$ and $\text{C}$.
    Assuming typical properties, the **Metal** is **$\text{B}$** (lustrous/hard). The **Non-metals** are **$\text{A}$ and $\text{C}$** (soft). (21 words)
  7. Graphite and iodine are non-metals but they shine. Explain it with suitable reasons.
    **Iodine** has a molecular structure reflecting light. **Graphite** has **delocalised electrons** in its layers, allowing light reflection, similar to metals. (23 words)
  8. Compare between metals and non-metals on the basis of their chemical properties.
    Metals form **basic oxides** and **cations**. Non-metals form **acidic oxides** and **anions**. Metals react with acid, non-metals generally do not. (24 words)
  9. Write any three points to show the importance of each: i) zinc ii) sodium iii) potassium
    i) **Zinc:** Galvanisation, immune function. ii) **Sodium:** Nerve impulses, fluid balance. iii) **Potassium:** Heart function, plant fertiliser. (18 words)
  10. Mercury and lead are harmful metals for human beings. How are these metals harmful? Suggest your views.
    They accumulate, causing damage to the **nervous system** (both), kidneys (both), and blood (Lead). View: Strict regulation and safe disposal are essential. (28 words)
  11. You are given a bulb, one metre copper wire, a switch and a battery. How could you use them to distinguish the samples of metals and non-metals? Could write the usefulness of this test in distinguishing metals and non-metals?
    Build a **series circuit** to test conductivity. If the bulb **glows**, it's a **metal** (useful); if it does not, it's a **non-metal** (useful). (30 words) [attachment_0](attachment)
  12. Compare the properties of a typical metal and non-metal based on the following: i) lustre ii) conductivity iii) ductility
    i) **Lustre:** Metal-Shiny, Non-metal-Dull. ii) **Conductivity:** Metal-Good, Non-metal-Poor. iii) **Ductility:** Metal-Ductile, Non-metal-Non-ductile/Brittle. (21 words)
  13. Make a list of any five sources and any two deficiency symptoms and importance of the following metallic minerals essential for human body.
    **Fe** (Sources: Spinach; Importance: Haemoglobin; Deficiency: Anaemia). **Zn** (Sources: Meat; Importance: Immunity; Deficiency: Slow healing). (24 words)
  14. If you are given two samples, how do you differentiate which one is metal and which one is non-metal?
    Test their **conductivity** (bulb glows for metal) and **malleability** (hammering: metal flattens, non-metal breaks). (21 words)
  15. You came to know that diamond is the hardest non-metal and mercury is a soft metal. What questions are raised in your mind?
    Questions arise about the **exceptions** to typical properties: Why is diamond so hard? Why is Mercury liquid? Are other physical/chemical properties also exceptional? (27 words)
