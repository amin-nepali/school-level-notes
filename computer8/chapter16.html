Short Answers: Differentiate Between (Question 4)

Here are the answers for the differentiation questions, presented in a concise, table format.

4. Differentiate between: ⚖️

a. Prokaryotes and Eukaryotes

Feature Prokaryotes Eukaryotes
Definition Organisms lacking a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Organisms possessing a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles.
Nucleus Absent. Genetic material (DNA) is in the cytoplasm (nucleoid region). Present. Genetic material is enclosed within a nuclear membrane.
Organelles Membrane-bound organelles (Mitochondria, Golgi, ER) are absent. Membrane-bound organelles (Mitochondria, Golgi, ER) are present.
Size & Complexity Generally smaller and structurally simple (unicellular). Generally larger and structurally complex (uni- or multicellular).
Example Bacteria, Cyanobacteria. Protists, Fungi, Plants, Animals.

b. Genus and Species

Feature Genus Species
Definition A group of closely related species. It is a higher rank in the hierarchy. A group of organisms that can interbreed and produce fertile offspring. It is the lowest rank.
Position in Hierarchy Above Species, below Family. The most specific, basic unit of classification.
Composition May include one or more different species (e.g., *Panthera* includes *leo* and *tigris*). Includes a set of individuals with similar, defined characteristics.
Scientific Name The first part of the scientific name, capitalized (e.g., **Canis**). The second part of the scientific name, lowercase (e.g., **familiaris**).

c. Protista and Monera

Feature Protista Monera
Cell Type Eukaryotic (have a true nucleus). Prokaryotic (lack a true nucleus).
Kingdom Kingdom Protista. Kingdom Monera.
Organization Mostly unicellular. Always unicellular.
Membrane Organelles Present (e.g., Mitochondria). Absent.
Example *Amoeba*, *Paramecium*, *Euglena*. Bacteria, Cyanobacteria (*Nostoc*).

d. Two Kingdom and Five Kingdom Classification

Feature Two Kingdom Classification (Linnaeus, 1758) Five Kingdom Classification (Whittaker, 1969)
Kingdoms Two: Plantae and Animalia. Five: Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, and Animalia.
Basis Primarily on the presence/absence of a cell wall and motility. Based on cell structure (Pro/Eukaryotic), body organization (Uni/Multicellular), and mode of nutrition.
Distinction Does not distinguish between Prokaryotes and Eukaryotes, or Unicellular and Multicellular forms. Clearly separates Prokaryotes (Monera) and distinguishes non-photosynthetic Fungi from Plants.
Acceptance Outdated and considered unscientific. Widely accepted as the most scientific and natural system.

e. Yeast and Mushroom

Feature Yeast Mushroom
Classification Kingdom Fungi (Ascomycetes). Kingdom Fungi (Basidiomycetes).
Body Structure Unicellular (single-celled). Multicellular (composed of an underground network of hyphae and a visible fruiting body).
Shape Typically oval or spherical. Cap and stem structure (umbrella-shaped fruiting body).
Reproduction Primarily by **budding** (asexual). Primarily by **spore formation** within the gills (sexual/asexual).
