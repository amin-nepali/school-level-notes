All alkalis are water-soluble bases. Not all bases dissolve in water to form alkalis.

üìñ Long Answer Questions (Max 30 words)

a. Write the physical properties of acids.
Acids are chemical compounds that taste sour when dissolved in water. They conduct electricity due to hydrogen ion presence. Acids turn blue litmus paper red, indicating their acidic nature. They react with metals like zinc and magnesium, releasing hydrogen gas bubbles. Acids corrode metals and have a pH value less than 7.
b. What are the properties of bases?
Bases are chemical compounds that taste bitter and feel slippery or soapy to touch. They conduct electricity in aqueous solutions. Bases turn red litmus paper blue, showing their basic nature. They react with acids in neutralization reactions, forming salts and water. Bases have pH values greater than 7 and release hydroxide ions.
c. Write the properties and types of salts.
Salts are neutral ionic compounds formed when acids react with bases through neutralization reactions. They have crystalline structure and conduct electricity when dissolved. Types include normal salts (completely neutralized), acidic salts (partially neutralized acids), and basic salts (partially neutralized bases). Salts are generally soluble in water and have various colors and tastes.
d. A solution turns red litmus paper blue. Briefly explain the nature of the solution.
The solution is basic or alkaline in nature because it contains hydroxide ions (OH-) which are responsible for turning red litmus paper blue. Basic solutions have pH values greater than 7. The hydroxide ions neutralize hydrogen ions, creating an alkaline environment. Common basic solutions include soap water, lime water, and sodium hydroxide solution.
e. Write any two uses of acids, and salts in everyday life.
Acids have numerous applications: vinegar (acetic acid) is used for food preservation and cooking, while citric acid is added to beverages for taste. Salts are equally important: table salt (sodium chloride) is essential for food seasoning and body functions, while calcium chloride is used for de-icing roads in winter and as a drying agent.
f. You have three test tubes: one contains saline solution, another contains lemon water, and the third contains liquid soap. You are given blue litmus paper. How will you identify which test tube contains the salt, the acid, and the base?
Dip blue litmus paper into each test tube separately. Lemon water will turn the blue litmus paper red because it contains citric acid. Liquid soap will keep the litmus paper blue or make it darker blue as it's basic. Saline solution will show no color change as salt is neutral, keeping the litmus paper blue without any chemical reaction occurring.
