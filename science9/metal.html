2. Short Answer Type Questions
What is metal? Give an example of a shining non-metal and an example of the best conductor of heat.
Metals are lustrous, malleable, and ductile elements that can conduct electricity and heat. They usually form positive ions called cations when they react with other elements. A shining non-metal is Iodine, which has a metallic lustre. The best conductor of heat among all metals is Silver.
What is the difference between conductor and insulator?
Conductors are materials that allow electricity and heat to flow through them easily, like copper and aluminum. Insulators are materials that restrict or block the flow of electricity and heat, like wood, rubber, and plastic.
What is a malleable metal?
A malleable metal is one that can be hammered or pressed into thin sheets without cracking or breaking. Examples include gold, silver, and aluminum.
What is metallic bond?
Metallic bond is the electrostatic attraction between positive metal ions and the surrounding sea of mobile electrons that move freely throughout the metal structure.
Name any two soft metals.
Sodium (Na) and Potassium (K) are soft metals that can be easily cut with a knife.
Which non-metal can conduct electricity?
Graphite, which is an allotrope of carbon, can conduct electricity.
What are the metals found as minerals in human body?
Iron, Calcium, Sodium, Potassium, Magnesium, and Zinc are essential metallic minerals found in the human body.
What is metallic lustre?
Metallic lustre is the characteristic shininess or brightness of metals caused by the reflection of light by free electrons on the metal surface.
Write any four plant sources of iron in human body.
Spinach, Lentils, Beans, and Tofu are good plant sources of iron for the human body.
Name two metals which are harmful to human beings.
Lead (Pb) and Mercury (Hg) are highly toxic metals that are harmful to human health.
3. Long Answer Type Questions
Enlist any five properties of metals and non-metals each.
Properties of Metals: They are lustrous with a shiny surface. They are malleable and can be beaten into thin sheets. They are ductile and can be drawn into wires. They are good conductors of heat and electricity. They form basic oxides when they react with oxygen.

Properties of Non-metals: They are generally dull with no shine. They are brittle and break easily. They cannot be drawn into wires. They are poor conductors of heat and electricity. They form acidic oxides when they react with oxygen.
Give an example of metal which: i) is a liquid at room temperature. ii) is a poor conductor of electricity. iii) can be easily cut with a knife.
i) Mercury (Hg) is the only metal that exists in liquid state at room temperature.
ii) Lead (Pb) is a relatively poor conductor of electricity compared to other metals.
iii) Sodium (Na) or Potassium (K) are soft metals that can be easily cut with a knife.
What would you observe when zinc is added to a dilute hydrochloric acid? Write the chemical reaction that takes place.
When zinc is added to dilute hydrochloric acid, we observe rapid bubbling or effervescence. This happens because hydrogen gas is released during the reaction. The zinc metal dissolves gradually in the acid. The chemical reaction is:
Zn(s) + 2HCl(aq) → ZnCl₂(aq) + H₂(g)
Write the equation for the reaction of: i) calcium and water ii) sodium and oxygen iii) magnesium and hydrochloric acid
i) Calcium and water:
Ca + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + H₂

ii) Sodium and oxygen:
4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O

iii) Magnesium and hydrochloric acid:
Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂
An element reacts with oxygen to give a compound with a high melting point. This compound is also soluble in water. Which element - calcium or carbon is it likely to be?
The element is likely to be Calcium (Ca). When calcium reacts with oxygen, it forms Calcium Oxide (CaO), which is an ionic compound. Ionic compounds have high melting points because of strong electrostatic forces. Calcium oxide is also soluble in water, forming calcium hydroxide. Carbon forms covalent compounds with lower melting points.
There are three materials A, B and C. Materials A and C are soft and B is lustrous and hard; but material B is dull and not very hard. Identify the metals and non-metals from A, B and C.
Based on the given properties, Material B is likely a Metal because it is described as lustrous and hard, which are typical metal properties. Materials A and C are likely Non-metals because they are soft and dull, which are characteristics of non-metals.
Graphite and iodine are non-metals but they shine. Explain it with suitable reasons.
Iodine is a non-metal that shines because it has a special molecular structure that can reflect light, giving it a metallic appearance. Graphite shines because it has delocalised electrons in its layered structure. These free electrons can move and reflect light, similar to how metals reflect light, which gives graphite its lustre.
Compare between metals and non-metals on the basis of their chemical properties.
Metals form basic oxides when they react with oxygen, while non-metals form acidic oxides. Metals lose electrons easily to form cations (positive ions), whereas non-metals gain electrons to form anions (negative ions). Metals react with acids to produce hydrogen gas, but non-metals generally do not react with acids.

iii) Potassium: Crucial for heart function and muscle contractions. Important component of plant fertilisers. Maintains proper cell function.

Both mercury and lead accumulate in the body and cause serious damage. They affect the nervous system causing brain damage and memory problems. They damage the kidneys and affect their filtering function. Lead specifically damages blood formation causing anemia. My view is that strict regulations should be placed on these metals. Safe disposal methods must be followed, and industries should find safer alternatives.
You are given a bulb, one metre copper wire, a switch and a battery. How could you use them to distinguish the samples of metals and non-metals? Could write the usefulness of this test in distinguishing metals and non-metals?
Connect all components in a series circuit: battery, switch, sample material (between two connection points), and bulb. If the sample is a metal, it will conduct electricity and the bulb will glow. If it's a non-metal, it won't conduct electricity and the bulb won't glow. This test is very useful because conductivity is a fundamental difference between metals and non-metals. It gives quick and clear results.

If you are given two samples, how do you differentiate which one is metal and which one is non-metal?
Test their electrical conductivity by connecting them in a circuit with a bulb. If the bulb glows, it's a metal. Test malleability by hammering them. If the sample flattens into a sheet, it's a metal. If it breaks or crumbles into pieces, it's a non-metal. You can also check for lustre and hardness.

4. Give Reasons (Concise)
Ionic compounds have high melting points.
Strong electrostatic forces hold positive and negative ions together in a crystal lattice structure. A large amount of energy is needed to break these strong bonds and separate the ions, which results in very high melting points for ionic compounds.
Metals are lustrous.
Free electrons present on the metal surface can absorb light energy and then re-emit it. This absorption and re-emission of light gives metals their characteristic shining or reflective surface, which we call metallic lustre.
Metals are ductile.
In metals, layers of atoms can slide over one another when stress or force is applied. The metallic bonds are flexible and don't break easily. This allows metals to be stretched or drawn into thin wires without breaking.
Lead is harmful to humans.
Lead is a toxic heavy metal that accumulates in the body over time and cannot be easily removed. It causes severe damage to the nervous system affecting brain function, damages kidneys, and interferes with blood formation causing various health problems.
Metals are good conductors of electricity.
Metals possess mobile or free electrons that are not bound to any particular atom. These electrons can move freely throughout the metal structure and can easily carry electric current from one point to another, making metals excellent conductors.
Metals are used to make cooking utensils.
Metals are good conductors of heat, which allows them to transfer heat efficiently from the flame to the food. They also have high melting points, which means they won't melt or deform when exposed to cooking temperatures, providing durability.
Electroplating of copper can be made on iron but not on silver.
Copper is less reactive than iron in the reactivity series, so it can form a stable protective coat on iron. However, copper is more reactive than silver, which makes it difficult to form stable plating on silver as the coating may not adhere properly.
Sodium is kept immersed in kerosene oil.
Sodium is a highly reactive metal that reacts vigorously with oxygen in air and moisture in the atmosphere. These reactions can be violent and dangerous, producing heat and potentially causing fires. Kerosene oil prevents sodium from coming in contact with air and water, keeping it safe.
