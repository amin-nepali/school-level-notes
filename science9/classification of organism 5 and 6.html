Combined Answers: Questions 5 & 6

5. Give Reasons (Brief)

a. The five-kingdom classification is more advantageous than the two-kingdom classification.

It separates life based on key differences: cell structure (prokaryotic/eukaryotic), organization (unicellular/multicellular), and mode of nutrition (autotrophic/heterotrophic). This corrected the misclassification of fungi and bacteria.

b. Classification of living beings is essential.

It organizes vast biodiversity for easier study. Classification provides a standardized naming system, helps identify organisms, and reveals evolutionary relationships among species.

c. Protists are more advanced than monerans.

Protists are eukaryotic; their cells have a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Monerans (bacteria) are prokaryotic and lack these complex, membrane-bound structures, making protists generally more structurally advanced.

d. Mushroom is a saprophytic fungus.

A mushroom obtains nutrition by releasing enzymes onto dead and decaying organic matter. It then absorbs the broken-down nutrients, fulfilling the definition of a saprophyte and acting as a decomposer.

e. We classify organisms.

We classify organisms to establish a universal scientific naming system and grouping. This helps avoid local name confusion and allows scientists worldwide to efficiently study relationships and evolutionary history.
