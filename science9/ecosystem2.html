  • Examples of five food chains are:
    • Grass ‚Üí Rabbit ‚Üí Tiger

    • Grass ‚Üí Deer ‚Üí Tiger
    • Grass ‚Üí Grasshopper ‚Üí Hawk

üçΩÔ∏è Primary and Secondary Consumers

  • Primary Consumers are defined as herbivores that feed directly on producers (second trophic level). Examples: Rabbit, Grasshopper, Mouse, Deer.
  • Secondary Consumers are known as carnivores or omnivores that feed on primary consumers (third trophic level). Examples: Frog, Snake, Tiger, Hawk.

üêÖ Effect of Removing the Tiger

  • Tiger is called an apex predator (top consumer); its removal causes a Trophic Cascade.
  • Increase in herbivore population: Deer and Rabbit numbers rise due to no predation control.
  • Overgrazing and decrease in producers: Grass population drops sharply, potentially destabilizing the ecosystem.
  • Impact on other consumers: Grasshopper and Mouse face competition; Hawk and Snake populations may decrease due to food instability.

üåø Type of Ecosystem

  • Based on organisms like Grass, Deer, Tiger, Rabbit, Grasshopper, and Hawk, the ecosystem is defined as a Terrestrial Ecosystem.
  • More specifically, it is known as a Grassland or Forest/Savanna ecosystem (producers: grass; consumers: herbivores and top predators).
