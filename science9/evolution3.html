Embryological Evidence: Early embryos of vertebrates like fish, birds, and humans show strong similarities, suggesting a common ancestry.

[Embryo Sketches: Fish | Bird | Human]
(Gill slits, tail, similar early structure)

Justification:

  • Early embryos of fish, birds, and humans are very similar.
  • All have gill slits (pharyngeal pouches) and a tail (post-anal tail).
  • These structures are functional in fish, but modified or vestigial in birds and humans.
  • Shows a common ancestral origin and evolutionary history.

ü¶¥ Question B: Based on Fossil Diagram (Archaeopteryx)

  • i) Organism: Archaeopteryx ("ancient wing").
  • ii) Bird characters:
    • Feathers for flight
    • Wishbone (furcula)
    • Wings (primitive structure)
  • iii) Reptile characters:
    • Long bony tail
    • Teeth in jaws
    • Claws on wings
    • Biconcave vertebrae
  • iv) Bridge animal: Possesses both reptile and bird characters ‚Üí evolutionary transition link.

[Forelimb Diagrams: Human | Bat | Whale | Bird]
  • i) Type of evidence: Morphological/Anatomical Evidence (Homologous organs)
  • Explanation: Same bone structure but different functions (grasping, flying, swimming) ‚Üí common ancestry.
  • ii) Similarity: Same basic structural pattern of bones despite functional differences.
  • iii) Bones in all forelimbs:
    • Humerus (upper arm)
    • Radius and Ulna (forearm)
    • Carpals (wrist bones)
    • Metacarpals (hand bones)
    • Phalanges (fingers)
  • iv) How it supports evolution:
    • Same pentadactyl limb plan across species ‚Üí divergent evolution.
    • Shows adaptation of common ancestral structures to different functions and environments.
