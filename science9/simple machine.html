  2. b. Efficiency ‚Äî is called the ratio of useful output work to input work (in %).
  3. c. Factors that affect MA ‚Äî are as follows: friction, machine geometry, effort position and wear.

‚úçÔ∏è Long Answer Type Questions (Section 3 ‚Äî p.160)

  1. 3.a A single fixed pulley gives no MA gain because it only changes direction of effort; it is used to make lifting convenient or to redirect force for safety.
  2. 3.b In block and tackle, MA increases with more pulleys because each added pulley increases the number of rope segments supporting the load, sharing the load among more tensions.
  3. 3.c Uses:
    • Inclined plane ‚Äî is used to load goods onto trucks and to design ramps for wheelchairs.
    • Pulley ‚Äî is used in flagpoles and wells for lifting and in cranes to redirect forces.
    • Wheel and axle ‚Äî is used in door knobs and rolling devices like carts to magnify force or motion.
  4. 3.d To lift same load with less effort on an inclined plane ‚Äî increase the plane length or reduce the slope angle so effort distance increases but required force decreases.
  5. 3.e Efficiency derivation (short): Efficiency = (output work / input work) √ó 100%. Output work is useful work, input work is effort√ódistance; ratio shows useful fraction of input.
  6. 3.f Wheelbarrow as compound machine ‚Äî is called compound because it combines lever (handles and wheel axle) and wheel & axle to make lifting and moving easier.
  7. 3.g Screw and wedge from inclined plane ‚Äî a screw is an inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder; a wedge is two inclined planes joined back-to-back to split or lift materials.
  8. 3.h.i Effect of friction ‚Äî Friction reduces useful output work, converts energy to heat, and thus lowers the efficiency of a machine.
  9. 3.h.ii Velocity ratio of a machine ‚Äî is defined as the ratio of distance moved by effort to distance moved by load in ideal (frictionless) conditions.
  10. 3.h.iii Use of pulleys in daily life ‚Äî are as follows: lifting water, raising flags, window blinds, clotheslines, and in cranes for construction.
  11. 3.h.iv Compound machines ‚Äî are defined as systems where two or more simple machines are combined to perform a complex task with mechanical advantage.

‚ÜîÔ∏è Differentiate Between (Section 4 ‚Äî p.160)

  1. 4.a MA and VR ‚Äî MA is load/effort actually obtained; VR is distance_E / distance_L ideally (no friction).
  2. 4.b Input work and output work ‚Äî Input work is effort √ó distance by effort; output work is load √ó distance lifted (useful work).
  3. 4.c Simple and compound machine ‚Äî Simple uses one elementary device; compound combines two or more simple machines together.
  4. 4.d VR and efficiency ‚Äî VR is geometric distance ratio; efficiency is (MA/VR)√ó100% representing losses (friction).
  5. 4.e Pulley and inclined plane ‚Äî Pulley changes force direction or multiplies force; inclined plane reduces required effort by increasing distance.
  6. 4.f Screw and wedge ‚Äî Screw converts rotation to linear advance; wedge converts applied force into splitting or prying action.

ü§î Give Reasons (Section 5 ‚Äî p.160)

  1. 5.a VR > MA because friction and other losses reduce actual mechanical advantage below the ideal geometric ratio.
  2. 5.b Efficiency never 100% in practice because friction and deformation always waste some input energy as heat.
  3. 5.c Input work > output work because some input energy is lost to friction and heat; output is only useful part.
  4. 5.d Easier to climb slanted slope because effort required is less due to longer distance but smaller force compared to vertical climb.
  5. 5.e Easier with three-pulley system because more supporting rope segments reduce the force needed for same load (greater MA).
  6. 5.f Wheel and axle is called a continuous lever because the wheel edge and axle center act like many lever positions continuously.
