Q. Question Answer (Key Points Only)
a. Define ecosystem. A community of living (biotic) organisms interacting with non-living (abiotic) environment in a specific area.
b. Name any two components of ecosystem. 1. Biotic components
2. Abiotic components
c. Define decomposers. Organisms (mainly bacteria and fungi) that break down dead matter, recycling nutrients.
d. Examples of top consumers in a pond. 1. Large carnivorous fish (Pike)
2. Fish-eating birds (Heron)
e. Mention any two abiotic components. 1. Temperature
2. Water
f. Examples of decomposers in grassland. 1. Bacteria
2. Fungi
g. Type of interrelation between sea sponges and corals. Mutualism (both benefit)
h. Role of algae in pond ecosystem. Primary producers, making food via photosynthesis at base of food chain.
i. Role of bacteria in grassland ecosystem. Decomposers and nitrogen fixation (converts air nitrogen to usable forms).
j. Define ecological interaction. Relationships/interactions between organisms in an ecosystem (beneficial, harmful, or neutral).
k. Type of interaction between goats struggling for grass. Competition (both harmed for same limited resource)

üìñ Long Answer Type Questions (Condensed)

a. Biotic & Abiotic Components

  • Biotic: Producers (plants), Consumers (animals), Decomposers (bacteria/fungi)
  • Abiotic: Temperature, Water, Light, Soil, Air

b. Why green plants are called producers?

  • Make own food (glucose) using chlorophyll, CO2, water, sunlight
  • Photosynthesis ‚Üí start of energy for all food chains

c. Key Terms

Term Definition
ProducerOrganism that makes its own food (e.g., plants)
EcosystemCommunity of living & non-living factors interacting
DecomposerOrganism that breaks down dead matter (e.g., fungi)
Food webNetwork of interconnected food chains
ParasitismOne benefits, other harmed (e.g., tapeworm)
SymbiosisClose long-term relationship between two species
CommensalismOne benefits, other unaffected
Trophic levelOrganism's feeding level in a food chain

d. Effect of Increased Tertiary Consumers

  • Over-predation on secondary consumers ‚Üí their population decreases
  • Primary consumers (herbivores) increase ‚Üí over-grazing
  • Producer (plants) population decreases ‚Üí ecosystem imbalance

e. Abiotic Factors Affecting Environment

  • Light (Solar Energy)
  • Temperature
  • Water
  • Soil

Temperature: Controls metabolic rate & enzyme function; life exists in specific temperature ranges; extremes limit species distribution.

f. Food Chain (Diagram)

Grass ‚Üí Grasshopper ‚Üí Frog ‚Üí Snake

Shows linear transfer of energy; energy lost at each trophic level.

g. Grassland Ecosystem

  • Dominated by grasses; few trees; moderate rainfall
  • Producers: Grasses
  • Consumers: Grazing animals (zebra, deer), carnivores (lion)
  • Abiotic: Moderate rainfall, fertile soil, seasonal fires

h. Impacts of Climate Change

  • Loss of biodiversity (e.g., coral bleaching)
  • Species shift (migration for suitable temperatures)
  • Disrupted cycles (plant flowering, animal migration)
  • Extreme events (droughts, floods, wildfires)

i. Effect of Migration on Ecosystem

  • Source ecosystem: Less grazing pressure, temporary food shortage for predators
  • Destination ecosystem: More grazing, temporary food for predators, adds nutrients through waste
  • Helps redistribute energy seasonally and maintain balance
